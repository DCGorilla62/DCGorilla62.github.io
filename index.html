I am a PhD candidate at The Ohio State University. My research is in experimental astroparticle physics, focusing on high-energy galactic experiments as a member of the High Energy Light Isotope eXperiment (HELIX) collaboration.



About

I am a passionate physicist specializing in astroparticle physics with a focus on high-energy cosmic phenomena. My research bridges experimental techniques and data analysis to explore the fundamental particles and forces in our galaxy. Outside of research, I enjoy hiking and capturing nature photography.

Intro

Research

My research focuses on the detection and analysis of high-energy galactic cosmic rays using balloon-borne experiments such as HELIX. By employing advanced instrumentation and statistical methods, I aim to better understand the sources and acceleration mechanisms of these particles.

Teaching

I enjoy mentoring students and teaching physics courses that emphasize both theory and practical applications. My approach is to foster curiosity and critical thinking through hands-on experiments and interactive learning.

Service

I am actively involved in departmental committees and outreach programs aimed at promoting STEM education. I contribute to organizing seminars, workshops, and public lectures that bring science closer to the community.
